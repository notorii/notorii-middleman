Bank of America

Complaints about banking software are commonplace. Often a labyrinth of information is expressed through a chaotic visual language. In lieu of whining about problems, we prefer to search for solutions. We chose to upgrade Bank of America for their bland identity and equally banal website.

Current Identity

The current identity is a hodgepodge of visual elements with a high volume of visual clutter. Heavy use of gradients, drop shadows, and glow effects creates a 2000's web 2.0 look. The existing logomark is soft, rounded, and at an awkward perspective. There is an overall lack of trustworthiness and reliability in the current design.

Proposed Identity

Our proposed logomark in a sharp, precise form made of right angles; a design that signals reliability and precision. We moved the primary brand blue away from the 4th of July popsicle color, to the true navy blue of the flag.

Akzidenz Grotesk harkens to the placards and signage seen in the banks of yesteryear, while remaining a fresh typographic choice. The color palette is pure americana, with a subtle orange tint lending a feel of patina. The NASA Discovery is the perfect metaphor for our design solution; retro futuristic. The proposed identity is both nostalgic, and cutting edge.

Current Product Design

Hitting the homepage feels like encountering a brick wall. Small and inconsistent typography, key functionality placed in text links, and copious square modules lacking a grid system, are the main culprits. It's difficult to parse the key user actions due to density of information and high cognitive load.

Product Design

Each page has a clear focus on the primary user action. We've made it simple to access these functions by removing the other elements vying for the user's attention.
